## CORE MESSAGE (the spine of everything)

> **You're probably more qualified than you think. My job is to help employers see it too.**

Every section, every CTA, every line of copy ladders back to this. When in doubt, ask: "Does this reinforce the spine?" If not, cut it.

## 5. LEAD MAGNET RECOMMENDATION

**Build this one first: "The 12 Reasons Canadian Employers Skip Your Resume — and How to Fix Each One."** (A checklist/short PDF.)

Why it's the highest-converting option for *you* specifically:
- It's built on your unfair advantage ("from someone who's reviewed the resumes on the hiring side") — no one else can claim that as credibly.
- It delivers an instant "*oh — THAT'S why*" moment, which is the feeling that earns trust and the next click.
- It's fast for you to create (you already know the answers), and easy to deliver by email.
- Every item naturally points toward a paid service ("Reason #7 is the hardest to fix alone — that's what the Resume Rewrite is for").

*Higher-touch alternative for later:* a limited number of free mini "Resume Red-Flag Audits" (they submit a resume, get a short personalized note/video). Converts extremely well and qualifies serious leads, but doesn't scale — use it as a scarcity offer, not the main magnet.

## 6. CONVERSION RECOMMENDATIONS

- **Two CTAs, two intents, everywhere:** low-friction (free checklist → email) for the 95% not ready to buy, and high-intent (book a consultation) for the few who are.
- **Make the lead magnet the hero's primary CTA.** Most first-time visitors won't book — but they'll trade an email for instant insight. Capture, then nurture.
- **Sticky nav with a persistent "Book a consultation" button** on every page.
- **Repeat the CTA** after the Hidden Gap, after Transformation, after the Resume Showcase, and in the Final CTA. People convert at different moments.
- **One form field only (email).** Every extra field measurably lowers signups.
- **Honest button microcopy:** "Send me the checklist," not "Submit."
- **Add analytics from day one** so we optimize from data, not guesses.
- **Build an email nurture sequence** behind the checklist (3–5 emails: deliver value → build trust → soft offer). I'll write this next.

## 7. TRUST-BUILDING RECOMMENDATIONS

- **Your face, prominently** (the headshot) — faces build trust faster than anything else.
- **Specific, verifiable authority** over vague claims: "I've reviewed resumes and supported hiring decisions" beats "career expert."
- **Use honesty as a trust device.** The "I can't guarantee a job" FAQ answer will build *more* trust than any hype could.
- **Zero fabricated proof — ever.** No invented testimonials, no made-up numbers. This is the whole brand.
- **Consistent premium design** (navy / gold / cream) signals seriousness.
- **Show the work** (the before/after resume) — demonstrated skill is the strongest proof you can offer before testimonials exist.

## 8. TESTIMONIAL LAYOUT RECOMMENDATIONS

- Premium card grid. Each card: photo or initial avatar · name *or* "Role, country of origin, year arrived" · the quote · and ideally a **specific result** ("3 interviews in 2 weeks after 6 months of silence").
- Mix formats: several short, punchy quotes + one or two longer "success story" features.
- **Video testimonials convert highest** — collect them as soon as you can.
- **Getting your first real ones (the beta plan):** offer 8–10 people a free or low-cost review over the next few weeks in exchange for honest feedback and permission to share results. This gives you real testimonials *and* real before/after resumes — and stress-tests your offer before you charge full price.

## 9. RESUME SHOWCASE RECOMMENDATIONS

- Side-by-side **Before / After**, with callout annotations (arrows/labels) on the specific changes.
- Each annotation explains **why it matters to an employer or an ATS** — that's what makes it persuasive, not just pretty.
- A before/after slider is nice-to-have; a clean static side-by-side converts just as well.
- Source ethically: anonymized real resumes (with permission) or a labeled illustrative sample you build yourself.

---

## 10. SOCIAL IMPACT — RESPONSIBLE IMPLEMENTATION

- **Phase 1 (now, sustainable from day one):** non-monetary giving — e.g., a set number of free resume reviews each month for people who can't pay, free public resources, an occasional free workshop. Real impact, zero financial risk.
- **Phase 2 (once revenue is steady):** a transparent, sustainable financial commitment — e.g., "for every paid client, one free review," or a portion of *profit* (not revenue). Avoid publishing a specific % until you know your numbers.
- **Be transparent:** a simple annual "here's what this made possible" note.
- **Partner with vetted newcomer-serving organizations** rather than going it alone.
- **Keep the tone humble.** Understate it. This builds more trust than a marketing badge ever could.

## 11. MOBILE RECOMMENDATIONS

- **Design mobile-first** — a large share of your audience will land on a phone.
- Single-column stacking, large tap targets, generous spacing.
- **Sticky bottom CTA bar on mobile** ("Free checklist" / "Book").
- Hero headline scales down gracefully; CTAs go full-width and thumb-reachable.
- Email capture must be one tap, one field.
- Compress and lazy-load the before/after resume images.
- Test the booking flow on a real phone before launch.
